Introduction

Welcome to Cyclistic bike-share analysis case study which is the capstone project for the Google Data Analytics certificate. Cyclistic is a fictional bike-share company that would use this analysis for a new marketing strategy.

Scenario

The director of marketing believes the company’s future success depends on maximizing the number of annual memberships. Therefore, our team wants to understand how casual riders and annual members use Cyclistic bikes differently. From these insights, our team will design a new marketing strategy to convert casual riders into annual members. But first, Cyclistic executives must approve our recommendations.

Stakeholders

Cyclistic A bike-share program that features more than 5,800 bicycles and 600 docking stations.
Lily Moreno The director of marketing and your manager.
Cyclistic marketing analytics team A team of data analysts who are responsible for collecting, analyzing, and reporting data that helps guide Cyclistic marketing strategy.
Cyclistic executive team The notoriously detail-oriented executive team will decide whether to approve the recommended marketing program.

The Business Task

The marketing team task is to make a future marketing strategy that would help converting casual riders to annual members, so we will analyse past data to find trends that would help us understanding how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently.

Data Source

We will use Cyclistic’s historical trip data for 12 months from 10-2021 to 09-2022 to analyze and identify trends. For the purposes of this case study, the datasets are appropriate and will enable you to answer the business questions. The data has been made available by Motivate International Inc. under this license.)

Insights

  • Casual riders made about 40% from Cyclistic’s total trips in the last 12 months.
  • Casual riders records higher average trip length during all the days of week.
  • Saturday is the mode day of the week per number of trips, and casual riders make more trips on this day than annual members.
  • Annual members make more number of trips during weekdays while casual riders make more during weekend days.
  • Total number of trips reaches its peak in the middle months of the year.
  • 18:00 o’clock is the hour with the highest number of trips, 08:00 comes second.
  • Classic Bike is the most demanded bike type and docked bike is the least.
  • annual members have not made any trips with docked bikes during the analyzed period.

Recommendations

  • Since the number of trips made by casual riders increases in the summer months we can put more marketing resources towards these months to attract more members.
  • Casual riders records longer trips so we can discuss some discounts on the long trips to attract the to buy annual membership.
  • Docked bikes are the least demanded so we should discuss a discount to attract more requests on it.
  • Peak hours show that riders have made more trips at hours before and after work so we should target riders who uses bikes to commute for their work.
